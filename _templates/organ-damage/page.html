Organ Damage

Organ by organ — the symptoms in the first weeks, what carries on through the first two years, and what damage is permanent.

← Back to Recovery Life Skills
Withdrawal can kill. Never quit heavy drinking cold-turkey without medical help. Seizures and delirium tremens are fatal. Call 911 in an emergency, or 988 for the suicide and crisis lifeline.
This is general information I gathered during my own recovery — not a diagnosis, a prognosis, or medical advice. I went to Gemini to help pick my own experiences. Healing depends on how much and how long a person drank, their age, genetics, nutrition, and other health conditions. Only a doctor can tell you where your body stands. What is written here is meant to give hope: a great deal of damage does heal once the drinking stops — and where a scar does stay, sobriety still halts it from spreading.

Permanent Damage
Wernicke's Encephalopathy: Life Threatening, Disorientation, Sluggishness, Apathy, Lethargy, Abnormal Eye Movements, loss of coordination, staggering walk, stance instability
Korsakoff's Syndromes: Amnesia, Misunderstanding of Time, Confabulation, Apathy, Lack of Insight, Disorientation
Brain Shrinkage: Impaired Judgment, Decision-Making, Loss of Impulse Control, Difficulty Concentrating, Chronic Unsteadiness, Fine Motor Decline, Slowed Information Processing, Short-Term Memory Lapses, Apathy, Mood Disorders
Cortical Thinning: Poorer Working Memory, Impaired Executive Function, Reduced Mental Flexibility, Accelerated Forgetfulness, Difficulty Recalling Words, Difficulty Reading, Slower Learning Curve, Spatial Confusion, Sensory Processing Delays, Increased Mood Volatility, Apathy
Cerebellum Damage: Unsteady Wide-Based Walk, Frequent Stumbling and Falls, Trembling Hands, Difficulty with Fine Motor Tasks, Poor Hand-Eye Coordination, Slurred or Halting Speech, Difficulty Judging Distance, Trouble Standing Still with Feet Together, Worse Balance in the Dark
Frontal Lobe Damage: Loss of Impulse Control, Poor Judgment, Difficulty Planning Ahead, Trouble Starting Tasks, Inability to Change Course Once Started, Blunted Emotions, Apathy, Socially Inappropriate Behavior, Irritability and Sudden Anger, Reduced Empathy, Difficulty Learning from Consequences
Returning Symptoms: Damage That Had Stabilized Can Worsen Again If Drinking Resumes — Confusion, Memory Loss, Balance Problems, and Coordination Loss Come Back Faster and More Severely Than Before
Correlated (Personal Experience)
Sleep Apnea: mental fatigue, mood changes, lack of focus, headaches, poor decision-making, memory loss, weakened immunity, brain fog, cognitive impairment, slurred speech. See Internal Medicine Physician, Primary Care Physician, Pulmonologist
Overweight: shortness of breath, joint pain, increased risk of heart disease and diabetes
High Blood Pressure: headaches, nosebleeds, shortness of breath, chest pain, irregular heartbeat
High Cholesterol: usually no symptoms at all, found only on blood work, narrowed blood flow to the brain over time
Heart Disease: chest pain or pressure, shortness of breath on exertion, swelling in the legs and feet, fatigue, irregular or racing heartbeat, lightheadedness
Diabetes: excessive thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, blurred vision, slow-healing cuts, numbness or tingling in the hands and feet, fatigue
Insulin Resistance: energy crashes after meals, intense sugar cravings, weight gain around the middle, brain fog, drowsiness after eating, darkened skin patches on the neck
Thyroid Issues: fatigue, weight change without diet change, feeling cold or overheated, hair thinning, dry skin, slowed thinking, depression, anxiety, tremor
Vitamin Deficiencies: B1, B12, folate, and magnesium are the ones alcohol strips — numbness and tingling, memory problems, confusion, fatigue, poor balance, sore tongue, irritability
Dehydration: headache, dizziness on standing, dry mouth, dark urine, muscle cramps, poor concentration, fatigue
Lack of Exercise: loss of muscle and balance, poor sleep, low mood, reduced blood flow to the brain, worsening of nearly everything else on this list
Other Prescription Medications: sedation, dizziness, memory problems, confusion — bring a full list of everything you take to every appointment
Psychiatric Issues: depression, anxiety, PTSD, and ADHD are common alongside drinking — low mood, hopelessness, racing thoughts, poor focus, insomnia, anhedonia
Previous Brain Damage: concussions, falls, overdoses, and seizures stack with alcohol damage — memory lapses, headaches, poor concentration, mood volatility
Low Testosterone: fatigue, low motivation, depressed mood, muscle loss, weight gain, poor concentration, low libido
Cushing's Disease: weight gain in the face and midsection, easy bruising, purple stretch marks, thin skin, muscle weakness, high blood pressure, mood swings, memory problems
Malnutrition: alcohol displaces food and blocks absorption — muscle wasting, weakness, poor wound healing, frequent illness, confusion, neuropathy
Poor Sleeping Habits: irregular bedtime, screens late, caffeine and nicotine at night — unrefreshing sleep, brain fog, irritability, poor memory consolidation
Oxygen Deprivation: from apnea, lung disease, or anemia — morning headaches, brain fog, poor concentration, memory loss, daytime sleepiness
Balance Problems: may come from the inner ear, neuropathy, blood pressure drops, or medication — not always the cerebellum, and worth a doctor sorting out which
Memory Loss: sleep loss, thiamine deficiency, thyroid, depression, and medication all cause it — several are correctable, so get it checked rather than assuming permanent damage
Anhedonia: flat, joyless, nothing feels rewarding — overlaps PAWS, depression, low testosterone, and poor sleep; usually lifts with time sober but is treatable if it does not
Trouble Reading: losing the line, rereading paragraphs, words not sticking — check vision first, then sleep, attention, and thyroid
My opinion is that if you are experiencing any of these, go to a doctor, call 911, or the ER.

Permanent Damage
Cirrhosis: scar tissue replaces working liver — fatigue, jaundice, fluid in the abdomen, swollen legs, easy bruising, itching, muscle wasting; the scarring does not reverse, but stopping halts its spread
Portal Hypertension: blood backs up behind the scarred liver — swollen abdomen, enlarged spleen, visible veins across the belly, low platelets
Esophageal Varices: swollen veins in the throat that can burst — vomiting blood and black stools are a life-threatening emergency
Hepatic Encephalopathy: toxins the liver can no longer clear reach the brain — confusion, personality change, day-night reversal, hand flapping tremor, drowsiness, coma
Raised Liver Cancer Risk: cirrhosis carries a lasting risk of liver cancer — ongoing screening is usually recommended even years after stopping

Permanent Damage
Alcoholic Cardiomyopathy: the heart muscle stretches and weakens — breathlessness, swollen legs, fatigue, irregular heartbeat; it can improve a great deal with abstinence but often leaves lasting weakness
Atrial Fibrillation: a permanently irregular rhythm — palpitations, fatigue, breathlessness, and a raised stroke risk that usually means lifelong medication
Heart Failure: the heart can no longer keep up — fluid in the lungs and legs, breathlessness lying flat, exhaustion on light activity
Raised Stroke Risk: from high blood pressure and irregular rhythm — sudden face droop, arm weakness, or speech trouble means call 911 immediately

Permanent Damage
Alcoholic Polyneuropathy: the long nerves to the hands and feet die back — permanent numbness, burning pain, weakness, and loss of balance; stopping and replacing thiamine halts it, but nerve that has already died back may not come back
Autonomic Neuropathy: damage to the nerves running the body's automatic functions — fainting on standing, irregular heart rate, sweating problems, slow digestion, bladder and sexual dysfunction
Foot Drop: the foot can no longer be lifted clear of the ground — tripping, a slapping gait, and reliance on a brace
Optic Neuropathy: damage to the nerve of sight — dimmed or blurred central vision, faded color, permanent loss of visual sharpness
Loss of Protective Sensation: injuries to numb feet go unnoticed — ulcers and infections that heal badly and need lifelong foot care

Permanent Damage
Chronic Gastritis: a permanently inflamed and thinned stomach lining — recurring pain, nausea, poor tolerance of spice, acid, and anti-inflammatory painkillers
Peptic Ulcers: open sores in the stomach or duodenum — gnawing pain, bleeding, and a risk of perforation that is a surgical emergency

Barrett's Esophagus: years of acid change the lining of the gullet — usually silent, needs monitoring because it raises esophageal cancer risk
Malabsorption: a damaged gut lining stops taking up nutrients — lasting deficiencies, weight loss, weakness, and anemia even on a good diet

Permanent Damage
Acute Pancreatitis: the pancreas digests itself — a medical emergency with severe pain, vomiting, and fever that can be fatal; every attack leaves scarring behind
Chronic Pancreatitis: repeated attacks scar the gland for good — lifelong pain, poor digestion, weight loss, and often long-term pain management
Exocrine Insufficiency: the pancreas stops making digestive enzymes — greasy stools, weight loss, deficiency of vitamins A, D, E, and K, usually needing enzyme capsules with every meal for life
Type 3c Diabetes: the insulin-making cells are destroyed — permanent diabetes that is often brittle and hard to control, needing insulin
Raised Pancreatic Cancer Risk: chronic pancreatitis carries a lasting increase in risk that stopping reduces but does not erase

Rhinophyma: the nose thickens, reddens, and enlarges from untreated rosacea — it does not shrink back on its own and needs a dermatologist or surgery
Spider Angiomas: small red spider-shaped vessels on the face, chest, and arms — a visible sign of liver damage that may fade slowly but often stays

Chronic Rosacea: flushing that becomes fixed redness and visible vessels — treatable but rarely reversible without help
Scarring: from falls, burns, fights, and wounds that healed badly while drinking

Permanent Damage
Most Blood Damage Reverses: counts usually recover within weeks to months of stopping once nutrition is restored — this is one of the areas that heals best
Macrocytic Anemia: oversized red cells from B12 and folate deficiency — fatigue, breathlessness, pallor; corrects with abstinence and vitamin replacement
Thrombocytopenia: low platelets from marrow suppression and an enlarged spleen — recovers unless liver scarring is driving it, in which case it persists
Clotting Failure from Cirrhosis: a scarred liver stops making clotting factors — lasting bruising and bleeding risk that does not resolve because the liver damage does not
Weakened Immunity: long-term drinkers carry a lasting vulnerability to pneumonia, tuberculosis, and sepsis that improves with sobriety but takes time

Permanent Damage
Osteoporosis: bone lost over years of drinking and poor nutrition — fractures of the hip, wrist, and spine from small falls; density can be stabilized and partly rebuilt, but not fully restored
Avascular Necrosis: blood supply to the hip bone dies — deep groin pain, limping, collapse of the joint, often ending in hip replacement
Vertebral Compression Fractures: spinal bones crumble — permanent height loss, a stooped back, and chronic pain
Alcoholic Myopathy: muscle fibers waste away — lasting weakness in the thighs and shoulders; much of it recovers with sobriety and nutrition, but severe cases leave permanent loss
Old Fractures Healed Crooked: breaks that went untreated or set badly — deformity, arthritis, and pain that stays

Where the Risk Sits
Mouth and Throat: alcohol and tobacco together multiply the risk rather than add to it — mouth sores, lumps, hoarseness, ear pain
Voice Box: hoarseness that does not settle, a lump in the throat, difficulty swallowing
Esophagus: food sticking, pain on swallowing, weight loss — risk is higher again in people who flush when they drink
Liver: nearly always arises in a cirrhotic liver — screening is usually recommended once cirrhosis is diagnosed
Colon and Rectum: blood in the stool, a lasting change in bowel habit, unexplained anemia
Breast: risk rises with any regular drinking, not only heavy drinking — a new lump, skin dimpling, nipple change
Stomach and Pancreas: persistent indigestion, upper abdominal pain, weight loss, yellowing skin
After You Stop
Risk Falls, Slowly: mouth, throat, and esophageal risk drops over years without alcohol, approaching that of a non-drinker after roughly two decades
Some Risk Stays: where cirrhosis has set in, liver cancer risk remains raised for life and screening continues
Screening Is the Point: these cancers are survivable when caught early and often silent until late — tell a doctor your drinking history honestly so the right checks get ordered
Wherever your body is today, stopping is never wasted. The short-term wins come fast, the long-term ones reward the work, and even where a scar stays, sobriety keeps it from spreading.
