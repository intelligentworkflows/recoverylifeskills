Organ Damage & Healing

What the body can heal from after drinking stops — and what it cannot — grouped by how long recovery takes and by the organ affected.

← Back to Recovery Life Skills
Withdrawal can kill. Never quit heavy drinking cold-turkey without medical help. Seizures and delirium tremens are fatal. Call 911 in an emergency, or 988 for the suicide and crisis lifeline. See my Week 1 medical detox story.
This is general information I gathered during my own recovery — not a diagnosis, a prognosis, or medical advice. Healing depends on how much and how long a person drank, their age, genetics, nutrition, and other health conditions. Only a doctor can tell you where your body stands. What is written here is meant to give hope: a great deal of damage does heal once the drinking stops.

Heals in the Short Term (days to a few weeks)

The body starts repairing itself almost immediately. Much of the early damage is inflammation and dehydration, which reverse quickly once alcohol is gone.

Stomach & Gut
  • Alcohol-irritated lining (gastritis) calms down
  • Nausea and acid reflux ease
  • Appetite begins to return
Skin
  • Redness, flushing, and puffiness fade
  • Rehydration restores tone and color
  • Dark under-eye circles lighten
Blood & Hydration
  • Electrolytes and fluids rebalance
  • Blood sugar steadies
  • Blood pressure often begins to fall
Sleep & Energy
  • Deep, restorative sleep gradually returns
  • Daytime energy improves after early insomnia settles
Liver (early)
  • Fatty liver (steatosis) starts to reverse
  • Fat can clear substantially within weeks of abstinence
Immune System
  • Resistance to infection starts to rebuild
  • Inflammation across the body drops

Heals Over the Longer Term (up to ~2 years — the PAWS window)

Deeper systems take much longer. Post-Acute Withdrawal Syndrome (PAWS) — the mood swings, cloudy thinking, poor sleep, anxiety, and waves of craving that outlast the first weeks — can come and go for months and gradually fade over the first year or two. This is normal, and it does get better.

Brain (PAWS)
  • Mood swings and anxiety slowly level out
  • “Brain fog,” memory, and focus keep improving
  • Sleep patterns re-regulate
  • Cravings become less frequent and less intense
Brain Volume
  • Some grey and white matter lost to drinking is regained
  • Much of the measurable rebound happens over the first year+
Liver
  • Milder alcoholic hepatitis (inflammation) can resolve
  • Liver enzyme levels move back toward normal
Nerves
  • Mild peripheral neuropathy — tingling, numbness in hands and feet — can improve
  • Recovery is helped by B vitamins, especially thiamine (B1)
Heart
  • Alcohol-related high blood pressure often keeps dropping
  • Early alcoholic cardiomyopathy (weakened heart muscle) can partly recover

Heals Long Term — With Treatment, Nutrition & Exercise

Some organs keep recovering for years, but only with active work: staying sober, eating well, replacing deficient vitamins, moving the body, and following medical care. This is where the daily practices in Things That Worked pay off.

Brain & Cognition
  • Memory, focus, and problem-solving continue to sharpen
  • Exercise, sleep, and B-vitamin nutrition speed recovery
Liver
  • Early scarring (fibrosis) can partly regress
  • Further damage stops as long as drinking stays stopped
  • Good nutrition and medical follow-up matter
Heart
  • Cardiomyopathy can keep improving with cardiac care
  • Weight-bearing and aerobic exercise strengthen the heart
Bones
  • Bone density can rebuild over time
  • Weight-bearing exercise plus calcium and vitamin D help
Muscle & Weight
  • Lost muscle rebuilds with protein and exercise
  • Metabolism and healthy weight can be restored
Nutrition
  • Deficiencies (B1, B12, folate, magnesium) correct with diet and supplements
  • Correcting them protects the brain and nerves from further harm

Permanent Damage

This part is hard, and it is honest. Some damage does not heal. But stopping drinking still matters enormously here — it halts the progression, prevents the next injury, and can keep a serious condition from becoming fatal. Sobriety is worth it even when a scar stays behind.
Liver — Cirrhosis
  • Scar tissue (cirrhosis) does not turn back into healthy liver
  • Abstinence stops it from getting worse and can prevent liver failure
Brain — Wernicke-Korsakoff
  • Severe thiamine deficiency can cause lasting memory loss
  • Alcohol-related dementia may not fully reverse
Nerves — Neuropathy
  • Advanced peripheral nerve damage can be permanent
  • Numbness, pain, and weakness may remain for life
Pancreas
  • Chronic pancreatitis causes lasting damage
  • Destroyed insulin cells can lead to permanent diabetes
Heart
  • Advanced cardiomyopathy and heart scarring may not fully recover
  • Some rhythm problems can persist
Cancer Risk
  • Alcohol raises lifelong risk of cancer of the mouth, throat, esophagus, liver, and more
  • Quitting lowers future risk but cannot erase all of it
Wherever your body is today, stopping is never wasted. The short-term wins come fast, the long-term ones reward the work, and even where a scar stays, sobriety keeps it from spreading. If you are just starting, my Week 1 page shows how I began.
