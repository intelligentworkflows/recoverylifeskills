How this site is intended to be used

This is a personal website. It is a first-person account of one person's experience with long-term sobriety. It is not a treatment center, a medical service, or an official AA resource.

What this site is

  • A personal account of tools and practices that have worked for one person in recovery
  • A starting point for people who want to explore the 12-step program
  • A collection of links to external resources provided without endorsement or guarantee
  • Anonymous — no accounts, no cookies, no tracking

What this site is not

  • Medical advice — nothing here replaces a doctor, therapist, or treatment professional
  • A crisis service — call 911 for emergencies or 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • An official AA publication or affiliated organization
  • A substitute for in-person support, sponsorship, or professional care

External links

Links to podcasts and outside resources are provided as a convenience. This site has no control over and makes no guarantees about the content at those destinations. They are not endorsements.

Privacy

This site does not use cookies, does not collect personal information, and does not track visitors in any way. There is no account system and no form submission.

Intended audience

This site is written for adults who are curious about recovery, considering the 12-step program, or looking for a practical framework to start. Anyone in immediate danger should call 911 or 988 — not this site.
